# Carrom Tournament Rule

Carrom is played with 9 white pieces, 9 black pieces, and 1 red piece (the Queen). The objective is to pocket all your assigned pieces before your opponent, but you must also cover the Queen by pocketing one of your own pieces immediately after. The game can be played in singles (2 players) or doubles (4 players).

---

## 🪙 Basic Setup

- **Board**: Square wooden board with 4 corner pockets.  
- **Pieces**: 9 white, 9 black, 1 red (Queen), and a striker.  
- **Players**: 2 (singles) or 4 (doubles).  
- **Arrangement**: All pieces are placed in the center circle, with the Queen in the middle.  

---

## 🎯 Objective

- Pocket all your assigned pieces (white or black).  
- Pocket the **Queen**, then immediately pocket one of your own pieces to “cover” it.  
- The first player/team to pocket all their pieces wins the board.  

---

## 📌 Key Rules

### Turns
- Players take turns striking from their baseline using the striker.  

### Queen Rule
- The Queen must be covered by pocketing one of your own pieces right after.  
- If not covered, the Queen is returned to the center.  

### Fouls
- Pocketing the striker - needs to put one coin back as penalty. If no coins, no penalty.
- Failing to cover the Queen (Queen is returned to the center).  
- Striker not touching both baselines when played.  
- Shooting incorrectly (e.g., not from the baseline).  

---

## 📊 Scoring

- Each piece pocketed counts as **1 point**.  
- The Queen counts as **5 points** (only if covered).  
- Loser’s remaining pieces are added to the winner’s score.  

---

## ⚖️ Winning & Match Play

- A **board** ends when one player pockets all their pieces.  
- A **match** is usually played to a set number of points (e.g., 29).  
- After 24 points, Queen points are not considered.  
- The player/team with the highest score at the end wins.  

---

## 🚨 Common Variations

- **Point System**: Queen = 5 points, each coin = 1 point.
